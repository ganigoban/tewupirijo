An Origin Story In The City Of Angels

In the sprawling metropolis of Los Angeles, where dreams are just as often shattered as they are made, Danny Masterson emerged from a family deeply rooted in the entertainment industry. Born in 1976, this young Angeleno quickly found his footing in the world of commercials by the age of four. Hollywood, after all, was in his blood. His mother, Carol, was a manager, while his father, Peter, was an insurance agent but had his fair share of dalliances with the acting world.

Perhaps most recognized for his role as the sarcastically endearing Steven Hyde on That '70s Show, Masterson etched his way into our collective memory during the late 90s and early 2000s. With his long locks and nonchalant air, Hyde wasn't just a character. He was an emblem of teen rebellion. Beyond this iconic role, Danny ventured into various other projects. He explored the world of radio, spinning tracks under his DJ moniker. Masterson also took on roles in movies such as Yes Man and The Bridge to Nowhere. While marred by controversies, his career showcases many characters that are hard to box into a single archetype.
