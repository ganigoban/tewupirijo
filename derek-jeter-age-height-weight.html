Derek Jeter Age, Height, Weight

Derek Jeter Age, Height, Weight: Derek Jeter, officially known as Derek Sanderson Jeter is an American former professional baseball shortstop, businessman, and baseball executive

Born on June 26, 1974, he developed a love for baseball at a younger age and was consistent throughout his career rising through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after professional baseball players.

As a player, Jeter spent his entire 20-year Major League Baseball career with the New York Yankees. He was drafted by the Yankees out of high school in 1992 making his debut in the major leagues at age 20 in 1995.

He later became the Yankees’ starting shortstop, won the Rookie of the Year Award, and helped the team win the 1996 World Series over the Atlanta Braves.

Jeter excelled during the team’s championship seasons of 1998–2000; he finished third in voting for the American League (AL) Most Valuable Player (MVP) Award in 1998.

In 1999, he also recorded multiple career-high numbers and went ahead to win both the All-Star Game MVP and World Series MVP Awards the subsequent year (2000).

Jeter consistently placed among the AL leaders in hits and runs scored for most of his career and served as the Yankees’ team captain from 2003 until his retirement in 2014.

Throughout his career, he contributed reliably to the Yankees’ franchise successes. He holds many postseason records and has a .321 batting average in the World Series.

Jeter also earned the nicknames “Captain Clutch” and “Mr. November” due to his outstanding play in the postseason.

A five-time World Series champion with the Yankees, he was one of the most heavily marketed athletes of his generation and is involved in numerous product endorsements.

Jeter was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in his first year of eligibility in 2020; he received 396 of 397 possible votes (99.75%), the second-highest percentage in MLB history and the highest by a position player.

He was the chief executive officer (CEO) and part owner of the league’s Miami Marlins from September 2017 to February 2022.

Jeter is the Yankees’ all-time career leader in hits (3,465), doubles (544), games played (2,747), stolen bases (358), times on base (4,716), plate appearances (12,602) and at-bats (11,195).

His accolades include 14 All-Star selections, five Gold Glove Awards, five Silver Slugger Awards, two Hank Aaron Awards, and a Roberto Clemente Award.

Jeter was the 28th player to reach 3,000 hits and finished his career ranked sixth in MLB history in career hits and first among shortstops. In 2017, the Yankees retired his uniform number 2.
