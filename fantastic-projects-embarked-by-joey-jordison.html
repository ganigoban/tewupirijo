Fantastic Projects Embarked By Joey Jordison

Joey Jordison was an American musician who died with a net worth of $1.5 million. Joey Jordison is best known as the original drummer and founding member of the heavy metal band Slipknot. He also formed several more metal bands, including Murderdolls and Scar the Martyr, and performed with Ministry, Rob Zombie, Otep, and Satyricon. Jordison was a member of the global death metal ensemble Sinsaenum at the time of his death in 2021.

What are some of the projects engaged by Joey Jordison?

Below are some of the career projects engaged by Joey Jordison

:

1. Martyr’s Scar

Details of a new band comprising Jordison, Jed Simon, and Kris Norris appeared in April 2013. Jordison had done most of the instruments in this project, and Chris Vrenna and an anonymous vocalist were to complete the keyboard and vocal work, respectively. Scar the Martyr was formed on June 21 with Henry Derek as the vocalist. Jordison announced the dissolution of the project on May 5, 2016.

2. Vimic

Jordison revealed on Sirius XM on May 5, 2016, that he had formed a new band named Vimic. Jordison explained Vimic was “still 100% active” in an interview with Wall of Sound in 2018.

3. Sinsaenum

On May 20, 2016, Jordison announced the formation of Sinsaenum, a new extreme metal band led by vocalist Attila Csihar (of Mayhem and Sunn O))) and keyboardist Sean Zitarsky (of Chimaira and Dth). Jordison on drums, DragonForce bassist Frédéric Leclercq on guitar, Stéphane Buriez from Loudblast on guitar, and Heimoth from the band Seth on bass completed the lineup.

On July 29, they announced the release of their debut album Echoes of the Tortured, and their first single “Army Of Chaos” was posted on earMUSIC’s YouTube page. On August 10, 2018, the second album, Repulsion for Humanity, was released.

4. Remixing and performances

Jordison worked on a remix of Marilyn Manson’s “The Fight Song” in 2001. Jordison also appeared in Manson’s cover of “Tainted Love” music video. Later that year, Manson stated that he had collaborated with Jordison on his album The Golden Age of Grotesque. Jordison had indeed worked on guitars, but the track was not included on the album.

Jordison participated in OTEP’s album House of Secrets in 2004, drumming on six tracks. Jordison performed on Puscifer’s album “V” for Viagra in 2008. The Remixes, which includes a remix of the song “Drunk With Power.”
